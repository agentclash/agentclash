# Governance

AgentClash is an open-source project stewarded by its founders. This document
describes — honestly and briefly — how decisions get made today and how that may
evolve.

## Stewards

The project is currently led by its three founders:

- [@Atharva-Kanherkar](https://github.com/Atharva-Kanherkar)
- [@AyushRajSinghParihar](https://github.com/AyushRajSinghParihar)
- [@Shubham2582](https://github.com/Shubham2582)

They are the maintainers of record and own the areas listed in
[`.github/CODEOWNERS`](.github/CODEOWNERS).

## How decisions are made

- **Day-to-day changes** land through pull requests with maintainer review.
- **Larger or contentious changes** (architecture, public API, contribution policy)
  are discussed in an issue or [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/agentclash/agentclash/discussions)
  first; the stewards make the final call and aim for rough consensus.
- **Contribution-policy and legal-process files** (CONTRIBUTING, the CLA, CODEOWNERS,
  the PR template) require founder review — see CODEOWNERS.

## Becoming a maintainer

There's no rigid checklist. Contributors who show up consistently — quality PRs,
thoughtful reviews, helping others in Discussions — may be invited to take on
triage and review responsibilities, and eventually commit/maintain rights. If
you're interested, start contributing and let the stewards know.

## Licensing & the CLA

Contributions are accepted under the project
[Contributor License Agreement](CONTRIBUTOR_LICENSE_AGREEMENT.md). AgentClash is
MIT-licensed today; the CLA preserves the option to relicense in the future. See
[CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for what the CLA grants.

## Future

As the project grows, the stewards intend to form a legal entity (the CLA grant
transfers to it once it exists) and to formalize this governance model. Until then,
this document is the source of truth.
